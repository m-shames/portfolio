# Solitary Confinement Research

Quantitative research on solitary confinement practices, policies, and reforms using statistical modeling, causal inference, and machine learning.

---

## Statistical & Causal Analysis

Statistical analyses examining solitary confinement practices in New York State using confidential administrative correctional data. Examples provided focus on evaluating the impact of mandated reforms in the *Peoples v Annucci* Legal Settlement.

### Methods 
- Generalized Linear Models & Nonparametric Regression 
- Regression Discontinuity in Time (RDiT)   
- Network analysis  
- Machine learning (random forests & neural networks)

## Litigation Support & Monitoring

Provided statistical analysis and data monitoring for civil rights litigation challenging solitary confinement practices in New York State.   

**Responsibilities:**
- Settlement compliance monitoring and reporting
- Data quality assessments and validation
- Statistical analysis for legal filings
- Annual presentations to NYS DOCCS leadership

**Deliverables include:**  
- *V.W. v. Conway*, 236 F. Supp. 3d 554 (N.D.N.Y. 2017)
  - Shames Decl. in Support of Plaintiffs Motion for Class Certification
  - Shames Decl. in Support of Plaintiffs Motion for Preliminary Injunction
  - Shames Decl. in Opposition to Motion for Summary Judgment 

- *Peoples v. Fischer* (*Peoples v. Annucci*), No. 11-CV-2694 (S.D.N.Y.)
  - Annual joint status reports on settlement implementation
    
*Note: Case details and underlying data are confidential.*
